# ScoreLab Product Operating Document

## Company Principle

Think Big. Build Small. Learn Fast.

ScoreLab should make ambitious product bets, ship the smallest useful version, learn from real user behavior, and improve quickly.

## Product Definition

ScoreLab is an AI career coach for early-career job seekers.

The first product is a resume feedback experience that helps users see their resume through a recruiter's eyes. The long-term product is a decision platform that helps users understand what to improve next across their job search.

## Target Audience

Primary users:

- University students
- Recent graduates
- First job seekers
- Early-career professionals with 0-5 years of experience
- People applying to many jobs but not getting interviews

Do not optimize the product for:

- Senior executives
- High-income professionals
- Very experienced candidates
- HR professionals
- Recruiters as the primary buyer

## Core Positioning

See your resume through a recruiter's eyes.

ScoreLab is not just a resume analyzer, ATS checker, or AI writer. It should feel like a smart recruiter plus a supportive career coach.

## User Problem

Early-career job seekers often do not know why they are not getting interviews. They may be capable, motivated, and applying often, but their resume may not quickly show the right signals.

ScoreLab reduces uncertainty by showing:

- What recruiters may notice first
- What may cause hesitation
- What to fix before applying again
- How to improve the most important parts

## Core User Journey

1. Landing page
2. Upload resume
3. Free recruiter-style preview
4. Unlock improvement plan
5. Full report
6. Download PDF
7. Take action

## Product Principles

- Reduce uncertainty.
- Give fewer, sharper recommendations.
- Never shame the user.
- Explain what matters and why.
- Keep the workflow short.
- Avoid dashboards, accounts, subscriptions, and admin systems until evidence supports them.
- Automate the full flow.
- Make each product module reusable for future ScoreLab engines.

## MVP Scope

The MVP should focus only on:

- Resume PDF upload
- Text extraction
- AI or mock analysis
- Free preview
- Polar Checkout
- Payment verification
- Full improvement plan
- PDF download

Do not add:

- Login
- User dashboard
- Subscriptions
- Admin panel
- Manual review
- Manual report writing
- Complex CRM or email automation

## Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if users understand the value quickly and some users are willing to pay $4.99 for a focused improvement plan.

Measure:

- Upload conversion
- Preview completion
- Checkout click rate
- Payment conversion
- Report download rate
- Qualitative feedback from target users

## Future Product Direction

ScoreLab can expand into reusable scoring engines:

- Resume Score
- Website Score
- LinkedIn Score
- Pitch Deck Score
- Startup Idea Score
- Job Match Score
- Interview Readiness Score

Each engine should follow the same product philosophy: help the user understand what to improve next.

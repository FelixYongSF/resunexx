# ScoreLab Design Operating Document

## Design Goal

ScoreLab should feel like a premium AI product and a supportive career coach, not a generic resume website.

The user should feel within five seconds:

"This product understands exactly what I am going through."

## Brand Personality

- Calm
- Confident
- Human
- Premium
- Encouraging
- Professional but not cold

Never feel:

- Cold
- Corporate
- Judgmental
- Flashy
- Generic
- Overly technical
- Childish

## Reference Principles

Use these products as directional references, not visual templates:

- Apple: premium simplicity, typography, restraint
- Linear: clean product interface, spacing, clarity
- Stripe: world-class layout and visual hierarchy
- Raycast: modern product feel and polished interaction
- Headspace: warmth, emotional safety, encouragement

Do not copy their layouts. Absorb their principles.

## Visual Direction

ScoreLab should use:

- Large confident typography
- Warm off-white backgrounds
- Dark ink text
- Restrained accent colors
- Generous whitespace
- Editorial layouts
- Product-led hero sections
- Soft, purposeful motion
- Clear hierarchy
- Minimal navigation

Avoid:

- Generic AI gradients
- Floating card clutter
- Feature grids as the main homepage structure
- Loud colors
- Excessive glassmorphism
- Decorative orbs
- Overly complex 3D
- Stock startup illustrations
- Resume-template aesthetics

## Layout Principles

- Design around the product itself.
- Let one screen communicate one clear idea.
- Use whitespace as an active design element.
- Create rhythm through spacing, typography, and contrast.
- Prefer editorial sections over marketing blocks.
- Keep navigation minimal.
- Do not overwhelm users with too many choices.
- Use cards only when they represent actual product surfaces or repeated content.

## Homepage Direction

The homepage should not look like a resume website.

The hero should present the product experience:

- Recruiter first impression
- Signals a recruiter may notice
- A focused improvement plan
- One primary CTA

Primary headline:

See your resume through a recruiter's eyes.

Primary CTA:

Analyze My Resume - Free

Support message:

No signup required.

## Motion Principles

Motion should be subtle and premium:

- Smooth fade-in
- Gentle slide-up
- Soft hover movement
- Light reveal transitions
- Calm loading messages

Motion should guide attention, not entertain for its own sake.

## Emotional Design

The product experience should follow this emotional path:

- Landing: "I hope this helps."
- Upload: "This feels easy."
- Loading: "They are actually reviewing my resume."
- Preview: "Now I understand the problem."
- Payment: "This is worth paying for."
- Report: "I know exactly what to improve."
- Final: "I feel more confident applying again."

## Accessibility And Responsiveness

- Mobile must feel first-class.
- Text must never overlap.
- Buttons must have clear focus states.
- Color contrast must remain readable.
- CTAs must be reachable and obvious.
- Layouts must not depend on huge desktop screens.

## Design Review Checklist

Before shipping a visual change, ask:

- Does this feel like ScoreLab, not another AI tool?
- Is there one clear action?
- Is the page calmer after this change?
- Does the user feel supported, not judged?
- Can a student understand the value in five seconds?
- Would this still feel premium in a year?
